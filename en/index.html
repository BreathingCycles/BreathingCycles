How breathing music works

From first breaths to deep calm — the mechanisms behind the experience.

Start guide

Slow, even breathing is the body’s fastest path to calm. Stereo motion from left (inhale) to right (exhale) helps your body follow without effort.

Why left → right

Inhale cues draw attention inward; exhale lengthens and releases. Gentle left-to-right movement supports a balanced, embodied focus.

Vagus nerve & balance

Slow breathing increases vagal tone — the “calm-down” pathway — easing heart rate and blood pressure while shifting toward parasympathetic rest-and-restore.

Neurology & HRV

When you inhale, heart rate rises slightly; on exhale, it falls — a pattern called respiratory sinus arrhythmia (RSA). Longer, softer exhales amplify HRV, showing nervous system flexibility. Music-guided pacing makes this accessible.
