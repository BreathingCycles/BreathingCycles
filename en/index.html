What is Breathing Music

Breathing Music began years ago, after I experienced my first panic attack.

I realized that by consciously adjusting my breathing pattern, I could prevent it from happening again. I began composing music with a slow, steady rhythm — a sonic anchor to guide my breath whenever anxiety arose.

The concept worked so well it became part of my daily life. Later, when someone close to me was struggling, I created my first breathing music album: 5 Stille Moments. It was pure magic.

Since then I’ve collaborated with, among others, Anette Aarsland, and composed many hours of breathing music — for her exercises and my own. The method is always the same:

  • Use headphones
  • Lie down or sit comfortably
  • Press play and let the music guide your breath
  • Inhale as the sound moves from left to right
  • Breathe through your nose, exhale naturally
  • Focus not on taking in more air, but on breathing slower
  • Let emotions come — tears, yawns, smiles, laughter — all are release
  • Practice gentle belly breathing — it takes time to strengthen the diaphragm
  • When finished, think of someone you love, and notice how you feel now

As a composer, husband, and father of three daughters, I often need to reset my mind and find focus again. Without this method, I’m not sure where life would have led me.

Made with love,
Øyvind

The Future of Breathing Cycles

Once, Breathing Music was a way out of a personal crisis.

Today it has grown into something much larger — a shared human need. Breathing Cycles began as a tool for inner calm but has evolved into an exploration of how sound, breath, and technology can restore balance in our lives.

It’s no longer just about slowing down; it’s about remembering how we are connected — body, mind, sound, and breath. The future lies in bridging art and science, people and technology, the outer and inner worlds.

Technology should not distance us from life — it should bring us closer to it. I believe that art, music, nature, and community will guide us forward. If this project helps even one person find peace, it has already fulfilled its purpose.

And perhaps this is just the beginning.
Breathing Music now has a physical home — an installation where sound can be felt as much as heard.
Inside this small breathing space, time nearly stands still.
Investors with both heart — and perhaps pain — are welcome to get in touch.
For relief where medicine does not reach — that is what I’m working on now.

If you’d like to experience one of my personal favorites, try 6:3 to 20:10 from the album Slow Breathing Meditation. It gently leads you from a natural rhythm to deep, slow breathing — down to two breaths per minute. Stop whenever it feels right, and simply rest in the calm that follows.

Learn the Technique

Put on headphones. Inhale through your nose, exhale through your mouth. Let the music set the pace.

1. Awareness

Place a hand on your belly and feel the movement as you breathe.

2. Choose your rhythm

Select a playlist that matches your goal. The tempo guides your breath.

3. Follow the pattern

Breathe with the flow. Start with 3–5 minutes and increase gradually.
