Immersive Stereo Breathing Guidance

Slip on your headphones and follow the gentle motion of sound: the soft white-noise "breath" glides from the left channel to the right, cueing you to inhale on one side and exhale on the other. This subtle movement trains your subconscious mind to stay with the pattern, so your body synchronizes effortlessly with every cycle.

Guided Breathing Techniques

Follow these simple steps whenever you need to reset, relax or energize. Each cycle takes only a minute.

Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)

  1. Sit upright with your feet grounded and shoulders soft.
  2. Inhale gently through the nose for a count of four.
  3. Hold the breath for a count of four, focusing on a calm mindset.
  4. Exhale slowly through the mouth for a count of four.
  5. Hold the breath out for a final count of four and repeat the cycle 4 times.

4-7-8 Evening Wind-Down

  1. Place one hand on your belly to notice the rise and fall.
  2. Inhale quietly through your nose for 4 counts.
  3. Hold your breath for 7 counts, letting the body stay soft.
  4. Exhale fully through pursed lips for 8 counts, making a gentle whoosh sound.
  5. Repeat up to 5 rounds to prepare for sleep or deep rest.

Diaphragmatic Morning Activation

  1. Stand tall or sit forward on the edge of a chair with an open chest.
  2. Inhale through the nose allowing the belly to expand for 3 counts.
  3. Hold briefly for 1 count to sense the expansion across the ribs.
  4. Exhale through the mouth for 5 counts while gently drawing the navel toward the spine.
  5. Repeat for 6–8 rounds and finish with a deep cleansing breath.

How often should I practice these breathing cycles?

Short daily sessions (2–3 minutes) build familiarity. Aim for a longer practice every week to deepen the benefits.

Can kids use the playlists?

Yes! Start with one or two cycles and encourage kids to describe how their body feels before and after.

What do I need to get started?

A comfortable seat, a timer or one of the playlists, and a willingness to slow down your breathing.
